# Slide Structure

## Default 6-slide format

Use exactly 6 slides for the first version.

1. **Hook** — stop the scroll
2. **Problem** — amplify pain or tension
3. **Discovery** — introduce the app/tool/idea
4. **Transformation 1** — first positive reaction
5. **Transformation 2** — stronger payoff
6. **CTA** — app/product name + action

Keep the same subject and same visual angle across all slides. Change styling, result, or progression — not the identity of the scene.

## Hook patterns worth testing

- person + conflict
- budget pain
- curiosity / self-discovery
- before/after reveal
- POV

Examples:

- "My landlord said I can't change anything so I showed her this"
- "POV: you have good taste but no budget"
- "I had to see what my room would look like if I actually committed"

## Things that usually underperform

- vague labels instead of reactions
- generic prompts with no scene identity
- hooks with no tension or character
- too much text on slide 1

## Caption guideline

Keep the caption conversational. Tell a mini-story, mention the product naturally, and do not overstuff hashtags.
